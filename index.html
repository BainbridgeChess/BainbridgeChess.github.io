Beginner Workshop 1 → Sign Up

For those with zero prior chess experience.

Students will learn the basics of chess in a fun and engaging setting. Covered concepts include piece movement, capturing, castling, en passant, stalemate, board setup, coordinates and chess notation, basic checkmates, chess etiquette, and sportsmanship.

As participants master each concept, they will earn their very own tournament chess set to take home!

Workshop meets once per week at Bainbridge Independent. Choice of Mon 4-5p, Tues 9-10a, etc. 8 weeks total: first week 9/10, final week 9/10, no class on 8/2/3/4.

Cost is $195 per participant. Class size capped at 10 seats. Seat is reserved upon $45 deposit with signup.

Our Philosophy

Our Instructor

Andy became hooked on chess as a high school freshman, and his love for the game only continues to grow. He is excited to share his passion and experience with students and adults of all ages, backgrounds, and skill levels!

Andy has created and led chess clubs and classes at the Avalon Public Library, e3 Civic High, and MIT, where he earned his bachelor's in mathematics with computer science after 3 years of classes. He is recognized as an expert player by the United States Chess Federation (with a rating of 2089) and can play a full game completely blindfolded.
