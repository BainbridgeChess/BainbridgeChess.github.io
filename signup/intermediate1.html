Intermediate Workshop 1 Signup

Who is this workshop for?

This workshop is for those who have tournament experience rated 400-900, or who have completed a beginner workshop.

For help understanding if this workshop is right for you or your child, please call our instructor: 619-363-2623.

Where and when do we meet?

Workshop begins the first day of December, 12/1. We meet once weekly for a total of 8 lessons, either Sat 6-8p or Sun 5-7p.

Address is 9723 Coppertop Loop NE, 98110. Enter at the SW corner of the building.

No class during winter break (Dec 19—Jan 1). Workshop ends by the first full week of February.

What happens in the workshop?

Each lesson begins with a puzzle that the group works together to solve. This daily puzzle contains key themes and strategic concepts that are expanded upon in an interactive lesson. Students have the opportunity to apply their learnings in annotated exhibition matches against one another, which we will analyze afterwards.

By the end of the workshop, students will have increased tactical vision and theoretical knowledge, including an understanding of how to leverage their strengths and improve their weaknesses in competitive tournament play. We aim to build confidence such that participants are capable and comfortable improving on their own through self-study and review of grandmaster games.

How much does it cost?

Total cost is $295 per participant. Everyone will walk away with a brand new chess clock, a personal notation booklet, and a year-long membership to the USCF.

After we receive your registration form, we will contact you to request a $45 deposit that reserves your participant seat.
